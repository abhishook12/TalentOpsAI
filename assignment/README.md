# Data Analyst Assignment — Marketplace Revenue & ROAS Optimization

Thanks for taking this on. This is a single, realistic business problem from the world of
**marketplace advertising**. It's designed to show us **how you think** — how you break a fuzzy
business question down, form and test hypotheses, and turn data into a recommendation you can
defend. There is no single "right answer"; we care about your **approach and reasoning**.

## Use whatever tools you like
We care about your **approach and problem-solving**, *not* the tools you use to get there. Do it
in **SQL, Python, spreadsheets, a written report, a dashboard — or any mix**. Whatever lets you
do good work and explain it clearly is fine. Just tell us what you used.

**Show your working.** Whatever your tools, include the artifacts behind your answer — your
queries, notebook, spreadsheet, or dashboard — so we can see *how* you got there, not just the
conclusion.

### Using AI is encouraged 👍
We **actively encourage you to use AI / LLM tools** (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot, etc.).
Working effectively with AI is part of how good analysts work today, and we genuinely appreciate
candidates who put it to good use. It's optional — if you'd rather work purely in SQL, Python, or
spreadsheets, that's completely fine and won't count against you — but if you're comfortable with
these tools, we'd love to see you use them.

> **One hard rule: if you use any AI/LLM at any point, you must include the complete, raw
> transcript(s) of those chats** — the whole conversation, start to finish, unedited. This is
> mandatory when AI is used. A messy, exploratory transcript is exactly what we want; please
> **don't** curate or clean it up.

(Not using AI? Then there's no transcript to share — just include your working as above.)

## How we evaluate
- **Problem-solving & business judgment (primary):** Did you diagnose the real drivers, test your
  hypotheses, handle ambiguity, and size the opportunities with sound, clearly-stated assumptions?
- **Communication:** Could a non-technical leader follow your reasoning, and can you defend it?

We judge the *thinking*, independent of the tools. A clear, well-reasoned analysis beats a
flashy one with shaky logic.

---

## Logistics
- **Time:** a focused day. Depth and clarity beat length — we'd rather see a few well-defended
  insights than twenty shallow ones.
- **Stuck on a definition?** State your assumption and proceed

Good luck — we're genuinely interested in your approach.
